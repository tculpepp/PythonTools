Computing systems have grown increasingly complex and will continue this path for the foreseeable future. At the same time, resources have become more expensive and more difficult to accumulate. Systems integration addresses these problems by combining subsystems into one system that functions together. The subsystems can be used for multiple purposes and may not even be sourced from a single organization.

The systems integrator brings together these systems using a variety of techniques including networking, integration of systems, business process engineering, and the development of specific systems used in the cumulative integration process.

The first step in systems integration is to develop the requirements for the systems. Requirements form the foundation of the entire integration project by defining the needs of the stakeholders. Requirements not only define what is needed, they also what is not needed. Requirements define specific functionality and other aspects of the system such as quality and performance. A solid set of requirements will shape the project and contribute to the overall success. Requirements are also crucial for a good design of the system which will be done in the next module, so do a good job now and the next phase will go smoother.
