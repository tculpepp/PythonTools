Module 2 - Background

System Design and Project Planning

Project planning is widely considered to be the most important part of a project. A large percentage of projects fail due to lack of proper planning. Project plans form the foundation for the steps required to successfully complete a project and the plans identify the resources required during each phase of the project. Schedules are used to determine the order of tasks and the times for completion. Without solid schedules, project managers lack the ability to identify problems in the execution of a project.

Contrary to popular belief, project plans do not have to be executed as originally composed. Project plans should be created with flexibility in mind because most projects will encounter problems along the way. Flexibility in a project plan includes identification of high risk areas and methods to address the potential problems. Successful projects are the ones that are able to solve problems.
